01What the data says

A research pass across the Kickstarter audio category forced four corrections to our prior judgment-based estimates. These are now empirically grounded.

Display-as-hook is the proven pattern
Two direct comps prove visual-spectacle speakers clear $250k decisively: Haloasis A1 ($345k / 518 backers at $599 EB) and MorningBlues SonicGlass A1 (live, multi-million trajectory). Lead with a visual hook, not audio specs.
Wall-mounted is NOT dead
Trettitre cleared $1,003,881 from 2,490 backers in April 2026 with a wall-mounted modular retro hi-fi system. Yanko Design is actively covering the decor-speaker trend. Wall-mount category is empirically alive — magnetic-mount path solves renter resistance.
MorningBlues is in our lane right now
SonicGlass A1 (the new MorningBlues product) is currently raising multi-millions in the exact transparent + display-speaker lane the studio was working in. Refined Cube is structurally contested. Light Box must differentiate visually (diorama scene ≠ floating lyric text).
Hardware-wrapper-for-software fails
Humane AI Pin: 90% sales miss, $850M→$116M valuation (86% loss), all bricked Feb 2025. Rabbit R1: 100k sold but only ~5k daily users. Concepts whose value is "software via dedicated hardware" carry empirical failure risk.

Strategic Flag
MorningBlues isn't a reference anymore — they're an active competitor. SonicGlass A1 is currently raising multi-millions on Kickstarter in the exact transparent + vinyl-soul + display-speaker lane the studio has been working in. Any concept that enters this aesthetic territory must clearly differentiate within the first 3 seconds. The Light Box's frosted-glass diorama (silhouette/scene performance) is distinct from SonicGlass's transparent floating-lyric text — but the lanes are adjacent and the differentiator must be sharp.
